Introduction to Tuberculosis Tulang in Singapore

Tuberculosis tulang, or bone tuberculosis, is a serious health concern that affects many people in Singapore. This infectious disease is caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis and can affect any part of the body, including the bones. In Singapore, tuberculosis tulang is a significant public health problem, with many cases reported each year. According to the Ministry of Health, Singapore, tuberculosis is the fifth most common infectious disease in the country, with an incidence rate of 34.5 per 100,000 population in 2020.

Causes and Risk Factors

Tuberculosis tulang is caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis, which is usually spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes. The bacteria can then enter the lungs and spread to other parts of the body, including the bones. People with weakened immune systems, such as those with HIV/AIDS or taking immunosuppressive medications, are at higher risk of developing tuberculosis tulang. Other risk factors include old age, poor nutrition, and close contact with an infected person. In Singapore, the risk of developing tuberculosis tulang is higher among certain groups, such as the elderly and those with underlying medical conditions.

Symptoms and Diagnosis

The symptoms of tuberculosis tulang can vary depending on the location and severity of the infection. Common symptoms include pain and swelling in the affected bone, fever, weight loss, and fatigue. In some cases, the infection can cause the bone to weaken and collapse, leading to deformity or disability. To diagnose tuberculosis tulang, doctors in Singapore typically use a combination of tests, including X-rays, computed tomography (CT) scans, and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scans. They may also perform a biopsy to collect a sample of tissue from the affected bone, which can be sent to a laboratory for testing.

Treatment and Management

The treatment for tuberculosis tulang in Singapore typically involves a combination of antibiotics and surgery. The antibiotics used to treat tuberculosis tulang are usually taken for a period of 6-12 months, and may include medications such as isoniazid, rifampicin, and pyrazinamide. In some cases, surgery may be necessary to remove infected tissue or repair damaged bones. The goal of treatment is to eliminate the infection and prevent further damage to the bone. In Singapore, patients with tuberculosis tulang can receive treatment at public hospitals, such as the Singapore General Hospital or the National University Health System, or at private hospitals, such as Gleneagles Hospital or Mount Elizabeth Hospital.

Prevention and Control

To prevent the spread of tuberculosis tulang in Singapore, it is essential to practice good hygiene and take steps to reduce the risk of transmission. This includes covering the mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing, washing hands regularly, and avoiding close contact with people who have been diagnosed with tuberculosis. The Ministry of Health, Singapore, also provides guidance and support for people who have been diagnosed with tuberculosis, including counseling and contact tracing to identify people who may have been exposed to the infection. Additionally, the Singapore Tuberculosis Elimination Programme (STEP) was launched in 2017 to reduce the incidence of tuberculosis in Singapore by 50% by 2025.
