What is the role of a Guardian in Canada?

A Guardian in Canada is an individual or organization appointed by the court to make decisions on behalf of a minor or an adult who is unable to make decisions for themselves due to incapacity, typically in matters such as finance, healthcare, and personal care.

How is a Guardian appointed in Canada?

A Guardian is typically appointed through a court application, where an individual or organization petitions the court to be appointed as a Guardian for a specific person, providing evidence of the person’s incapacity and the proposed Guardian’s ability to act in their best interests.

What are the responsibilities of a Guardian in Canada?

The responsibilities of a Guardian in Canada include making decisions about the person’s finances, healthcare, living arrangements, and personal care, as well as managing their assets and ensuring their overall well-being, while also respecting the person’s autonomy and rights as much as possible.

Can a Guardian be held liable for their actions in Canada?

Yes, a Guardian in Canada can be held liable for their actions if they fail to act in the best interests of the person they are responsible for, or if they breach their fiduciary duties, such as mismanaging the person’s finances or neglecting their care, and may be subject to court oversight and potentially removed as Guardian.
