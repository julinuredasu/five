Understanding Sone 436: Perceived Loudness Metrics

The sone is a unit of perceived loudness, established to quantify how loud a sound is heard by a listener. A specific value, such as four hundred thirty-six, signifies a particular magnitude on this psychoacoustic scale. This metric directly relates to human auditory sensation, where a sound assessed at this level is subjectively experienced as being four hundred thirty-six times as loud as a reference tone (typically a 1 kHz tone at 40 dB sound pressure level). Such a measurement often characterizes the acoustic output of equipment, the ambient noise in a specific environment, or the performance of audio systems, providing a concrete measure of subjective auditory experience.
