Frequently Asked Questions

What are the symptoms of tuberculosis (TB) that I should look out for in Singapore?

Tuberculosis symptoms can include a persistent cough that lasts for more than two weeks, coughing up blood or phlegm, chest pain, fatigue, weight loss, and night sweats. If you experience any of these symptoms, you should consult a doctor immediately.

How is tuberculosis (TB) diagnosed in Singapore?

Tuberculosis is typically diagnosed through a combination of chest X-rays, sputum tests, and blood tests. In Singapore, doctors may also use more advanced tests such as the GeneXpert MTB/RIF test or the interferon-gamma release assay (IGRA) to confirm the diagnosis.

What are the treatment options for tuberculosis (TB) available in Singapore?

The standard treatment for tuberculosis in Singapore is a combination of antibiotics taken for at least six months. The specific antibiotics used may vary depending on the severity of the disease and the patient’s medical history. In some cases, patients may need to be hospitalized for treatment, especially if they have multidrug-resistant TB.

Are there any support services or resources available for tuberculosis (TB) patients in Singapore?

Yes, there are several support services and resources available for tuberculosis patients in Singapore, including counseling services, patient support groups, and financial assistance programs. The Singapore Tuberculosis Elimination Programme and the National Tuberculosis Control Unit also provide resources and support for patients, including free treatment and medication for those who are eligible.
