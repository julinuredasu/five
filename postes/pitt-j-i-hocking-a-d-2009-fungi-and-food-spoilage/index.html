The Role of Fungi in Food Spoilage

Fungi are ubiquitous in the environment and can be found in almost every ecosystem. They are capable of growing on a wide range of substrates, including fruits, vegetables, meats, and dairy products. In the UK, the most common fungal species responsible for food spoilage are Aspergillus, Penicillium, and Fusarium. These fungi can produce mycotoxins, which are toxic compounds that can pose a risk to human health. For example, Aspergillus flavus can produce aflatoxins, which are known to be carcinogenic.

In the UK, some of the most common foods affected by fungal spoilage include bread, dairy products, and fresh produce.
